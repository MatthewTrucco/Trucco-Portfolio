CoHabit

Project Objectives

  1. Evaluate the usability of Cohabit for household organization tasks.
  2. Identify any pain points, usability issues, or areas for improvement.
  3. Gather feedback on user satisfaction and overall experience.
  4. Validate whether Cohabit meets the needs and expectations of its target users.

Methods Used

  1. Moderated Usability Testing:
    • Conduct one-on-one sessions with participants.
    • Utilize screen recording software to capture participant interactions and feedback.
    • Allow participants to perform the setup of organizing a household and assigning tasks using Cohabit.
    • Encourage participants to think aloud to understand their thoughts and decision-making process.
    • Ask open-ended questions to gather qualitative feedback on their experience.
  2. Task Scenarios:
    • Create a set of realistic scenarios representing typical household organization tasks. For example:
      • Add a new task to the shared calendar.
      • Create a shopping list with specific items.
      • Assign chores to specific rooms and family members.
      • Set up notifications for important events.
    • Ensure tasks cover a range of features and functionalities in Cohabit.
  3. Post-Task Questionnaire:
    • Administer a questionnaire after each task to gather specific feedback. Include questions such as:
      • Ease of completing the task.
      • Clarity of instructions and interface elements.
      • Satisfaction with the overall experience.
      • Suggestions for improvement.
  4. System Usability Scale (SUS):
    • Use the SUS questionnaire to obtain a standardized measure of usability across participants.
    • Administer the SUS questionnaire at the end of the testing sessions.
